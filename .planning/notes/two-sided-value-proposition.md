# Two-Sided Value Proposition

## Consumer Side (Fee Scout Experience)

**Value prop:** Democratize financial intelligence. Help consumers understand if they're banking with a community partner or a problematic institution -- using government data nobody knows exists.

**Anchor:** Fee Scout institution lookup. Look up your bank, see:
- Fees benchmarked against district and national averages
- Complaints benchmarked (context does the work, not judgment -- "120 complaints vs 5,000 district average")
- Financial health from Call Reports translated into plain language
- "Why does this matter?" educational framing throughout

**Supporting pillars:**
- Consumer guides (overdraft explained, how to avoid fees, product comparisons)
- Comparison tools (local/regional peer banks)
- Research pages (state, district, national)

**Landing page:** Value-prop-first, not a split-panel chooser. Clear pathways: look up your bank, browse fees, read guides.

**Design tone:** Warm, editorial, Newsreader serif, terracotta accents. Informative, not data-dense.

## B2B Side (Pro/Premium Experience)

**Value prop:** Replace the $15K consulting engagement with a $2,500/year subscription. One portal that consolidates fee schedules, Call Reports, FRED, Beige Book, and complaint data -- with an AI consultant on top.

**Landing page:** Clean launchpad with four doors:
1. **Hamilton** -- on-demand AI consulting (the killer feature)
2. **Peer Builder** -- custom competitive benchmarking
3. **Reports & Studies** -- pre-built consulting-grade content
4. **Federal Data** -- Call Reports, Beige Book, FRED, complaints

**Hamilton positioning:** Not a chatbot. An on-demand replacement for Cornerstone Advisors, Ron Shevlin, etc. "Why pay $15K for a pricing study when Hamilton answers that in 30 seconds with live data?"

**Hamilton scope for pro users:**
- CAN: Generate scoped reports (peer briefs, annual fee summaries, competitive snapshots, 3-5 pages)
- CANNOT: Produce flagship national reports (that's Bank Fee Index's IP and content product)

**Personalization:** Account profile (institution, type, tier, state) should drive the experience:
- Their Call Reports ready on login
- Their district Beige Book highlighted
- Their competitive landscape pre-loaded

## Key Structural Changes Needed

- Consumer and B2B need distinct navigation and landing experiences (currently ~60-70% overlap)
- Pro users need expanded Hamilton capabilities (scoped report generation, not just chat)
- Institution pages need to become rich educational experiences
- Account profile data needs to actually drive personalization
- The split-panel gateway page should be replaced with a value-prop consumer landing
