# NATIONAL FEE INDEX — REPORT REDESIGN SPEC (V3)

## Objective
Transform the National Fee Index from a descriptive analytics report into a **decision-driving industry benchmark product**.

This report must:
- Influence executive decision-making (pricing, strategy, product design)
- Differentiate from generic "data reports"
- Be visually compelling, skimmable, and insight-dense
- Leverage ALL available datasets (fees + FDIC + NCUA + call reports)

---

# CORE PRINCIPLES

## 1. This is NOT a data report
This is:
- A strategic intelligence product
- A narrative-driven benchmark
- A "what should I do next?" engine

## 3. Visual > Tables

- 60% visuals

- Strategic themes

# CHAPTER 1 -- THE ILLUSION OF FEE DIFFERENTIATION

## Goal:
Show that pricing is tightly clustered -- not a differentiator

## Data Needed:
- Median, P25, P75 across all categories
- Standard deviation per category

## Visuals:
1. Fee distribution bands (box plots)
2. "Clustering chart" showing narrow spreads
3. Top 10 most commoditized fees

## Key Insight:
"80% of fees fall within tight ranges -- pricing is not a competitive lever"

## So What Section:
- Stop competing on price alone
- Focus shifts to experience, packaging, targeting

---

# CHAPTER 2 -- BANKS VS CREDIT UNIONS: TWO MODELS

## Goal:
Define strategic differences between institutions

## Data Needed:
- Bank vs CU medians (existing)
- Volume of fees charged (from call reports if possible)
- Fee adoption rates

## Visuals:
1. Side-by-side bar charts (bank vs CU)
2. "Premium gap" visualization
3. Heatmap of categories where each wins

## Key Insights:
- Banks monetize convenience (research, wires)
- Credit unions monetize behavior (NSF, penalties)

## So What:
- Banks: justify premium or lose ground
- CUs: risk regulatory exposure from penalty reliance

---

# CHAPTER 3 -- WHERE THE MONEY ACTUALLY COMES FROM

## Goal:
Move from pricing -- revenue reality

## Data Integration REQUIRED:
- FDIC Call Reports (service charge income)
- NCUA 5300 data
- Fee categories mapped to revenue buckets

## Outputs:
- % of total fee revenue by category
- Revenue concentration analysis

## Visuals:
1. Revenue concentration pie chart
2. Top 5 fee drivers (bar chart)
3. Long-tail distribution chart

## Key Insight:
"80% of fee revenue comes from X categories"

## So What:
- Optimize high-impact fees
- Ignore low-impact noise

---

# CHAPTER 4 -- THE INDUSTRY BLIND SPOT
