FFIEC Call Reports, Q1 2026
Published fee amounts suggest remarkable stability across American banking. The median overdraft fee has held at $29.00 for three consecutive quarters. NSF fees remain anchored at $25.00.
But Call Report data tells a different story. Aggregate service charge revenue on deposit accounts reached $7.2 billion in Q1, up 4.2% year-over-year. The divergence points to transaction volume — not pricing — as the primary revenue growth engine.
Key Finding
Eight of twelve Federal Reserve districts report rising consumer delinquencies, suggesting overdraft volume growth may accelerate through year-end.
For bank executives, the implication is clear: fee schedule stability masks growing revenue sensitivity to economic conditions. Institutions in districts with elevated financial stress should prepare for regulatory scrutiny of overdraft practices.
