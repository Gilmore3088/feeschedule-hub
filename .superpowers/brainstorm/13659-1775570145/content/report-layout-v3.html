Published fee amounts suggest remarkable stability across American banking. The median overdraft fee has held at $29.00 for three consecutive quarters, and NSF fees remain anchored at $25.00 across 2,049 institutions. For the 15 spotlight and core fee categories tracked by Bank Fee Index, median prices moved less than 2% in any direction.
But Call Report data tells a different story. Aggregate service charge revenue on deposit accounts reached $7.2 billion in Q1 2026, up 4.2% year-over-year. The divergence points to transaction volume — not pricing — as the primary revenue growth engine. Consumers under financial strain trigger more overdraft events, more NSF returns, and more penalty-generating activity — all at the same published fee rate.
Key Finding
Eight of twelve Federal Reserve districts report rising consumer delinquencies, suggesting overdraft volume growth may accelerate through year-end. Fee prices are stable. Fee revenue is not.
For bank executives, the implication is clear: fee schedule stability masks growing revenue sensitivity to economic conditions. Institutions in districts with elevated financial stress should prepare for heightened regulatory scrutiny of overdraft and NSF practices — particularly as the CFPB finalizes its junk fee rulemaking.
